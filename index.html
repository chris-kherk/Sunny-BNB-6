Managing Risk For Your Short Term Rental Property

Sunny BNB

For short-term rental property management, navigating the complexities of risk management is essential for both the protection of the property manager's business and the property owner's assets. Effective risk management encompasses a broad spectrum of concerns, including the safeguarding of property, revenue, cash flow, reputation, occupancy rates, and annual payments. Here's an overview of the risks that we see as vital in managing the risks for each short-term rental property:

Platform Risk

  • The digital landscape is ever-evolving, with changes to Google's algorithms and Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) presenting a significant platform risk. Dependence on these platforms can lead to increased fees and a loss of control over one's business destiny. To mitigate this risk, it's crucial for property managers to develop their own booking sites and strategies to increase the percentage of direct bookings, ensuring greater autonomy and financial stability.

Insurance Risk

  • Adequate insurance coverage is non-negotiable, encompassing building, contents, and public liability. Implementing procedures for thorough documentation, such as notes, photos, and messages, is vital for substantiating claims of damage. Timely claim processing and evidence submission protect both the property owner and manager. Of note is the requirement that a manager must have a current real estate license, which could expose the owner to rejection of claims.

Guest Risk

  • Guest-related risks include issues with identity verification, credit card fraud, unauthorized parties or events, and negative reviews. These concerns necessitate rigorous screening processes and clear communication channels to preempt potential problems and safeguard the property and owners assets.

Cleaning Risk

  • The reliability of cleaning services is another critical area. To address challenges such as cleaner availability and quality assurance, transitioning from external cleaning services to an in-house cleaning staff can offer more consistent and controllable outcomes.

Zoning/Planning Risk

  • Local council regulations and neighbor complaints pose zoning and planning risks for short-term rental properties. Establishing proactive communication strategies with neighbors and maintaining meticulous records can defend against unjustified complaints and regulatory challenges.

Maintenance Risk

  • Ensuring the availability of emergency maintenance tasks, like electrical and plumbing, minimizes property downtime and enhances guest satisfaction. A broad network of tradespeople supports swift maintenance responses and top-tier guest experiences.

Asset Protection Risk

  • Protecting the property's physical assets against theft and damage is paramount. A rigorous photo documentation process following each cleaning and all maintenance visits helps in monitoring the property's condition and addressing any issues promptly.

Payments And Financial Risk

  • Securing all booking-related payments requires robust systems, especially for platforms like Airbnb, which handles payments directly, and others like Booking.com and VRBO, where additional verification steps are needed to prevent chargebacks. Ensuring all funds are deposited into a registered trust account, subject to regular audits, is essential for legal compliance and financial integrity.
  • Navigating the complexities of short-term rental property management demands a comprehensive risk management strategy. By addressing platform dependency, insurance adequacy, guest behavior, cleaning services, zoning regulations, maintenance needs, asset protection, and financial transactions, property managers can safeguard their interests and those of the property owners, ensuring a thriving business model built on trust, reliability, and legal compliance.

Pool / Spa Certification Risk

  • Property owners are well aware that every pool and spa needs certification. What you may not be aware of is that each compliance certification is only current for 12 months. That means that every pool and spa needs to be recertified every year. The risk for this is if there was any accident or incident and the certification was not current, it could result in civil or criminal charges, plus a breach of insurance cover.
